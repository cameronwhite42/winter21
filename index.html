Crested Duck

Quack. Quack. Quack.

The Crested is a breed of domestic duck. It was probably brought to Europe from the East Indies by Dutch ships. It has its appearance because it is heterozygous for a genetic mutation causing a deformity of the skull.

What We Do


Crested ducks are seen in seventeenth-century paintings such as those of Melchior d'Hondecoeter and Jan Steen.

Crested Ducks like to swim in the water.

The offspring of a duck with a crest and a duck with no crest are expected to be 50% with and 50% without a crest.

50% are heterozygous for this gene and hatch with a crest of varying sizes.
